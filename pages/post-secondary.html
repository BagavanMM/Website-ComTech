Post-Secondary Pathway

Computer Engineering, University of Waterloo

The program is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of both computer science and electrical engineering principles. Waterloo's program also has a strong emphasis on problem-solving.

    Prequisites:

  • Advanced Functions. (MHF4U)
  • Calculus and Vectors. (MCV4U)
  • Chemistry. (SCH4U)
  • Physics. (SPH4U)
  • English. (ENG4U)
The pros of the program
  • Academic Excellence: The University of Waterloo is renowned for its strong engineering programs and consistently ranks among the top engineering schools in Canada and worldwide.
  • Co-op Opportunities: The university offers an extensive co-operative education program that provides students with valuable work experience in relevant industries, enhancing their skills and employability.
  • Engineering Focus: The Computer Engineering program at the University of Waterloo offers a comprehensive curriculum that combines both computer science and electrical engineering courses, providing students with a solid foundation in both disciplines.
  • Research Opportunities: The university is known for its cutting-edge research initiatives, offering students the chance to engage in innovative research projects alongside renowned faculty members.
  • Networking and Industry Connections: The university has strong ties with industry partners, providing students with networking opportunities, industry-sponsored projects, and potential job placements.
The cons of the program
  • Competitive Admission: The Computer Engineering program at the University of Waterloo is highly competitive, with a limited number of available spots. Meeting the minimum admission requirements does not guarantee acceptance.
  • Rigorous Curriculum: The program can be academically challenging, requiring dedication and hard work to succeed. Students should be prepared for a demanding workload and rigorous coursework.
  • Location: The University of Waterloo is located in Waterloo, Ontario, which may not be the preferred location for individuals seeking a more vibrant urban setting.

What about Job Opportunities?

Because of the versatility of a computer engineering degree, you have a wide range of different job opportunities ranging from software to hardware.

Software Engineer

Design, develop, and test software systems, applications, and algorithms for various industries, including software development companies, technology firms, and research organizations.

Hardware Engineer

Design, analyze, and develop computer hardware components and systems, working on tasks such as circuit design, microprocessor development, and embedded systems programming.

Systems Engineer

Design and implement complex computer systems, networks, and infrastructure, ensuring optimal performance, scalability, and reliability.

Embedded Systems Engineer

Develop and program embedded systems, such as those used in consumer electronics, automotive systems, medical devices, or industrial automation.

Network Engineer

Design, configure, and maintain computer networks for organizations, ensuring smooth communication, data transfer, and security.

Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning Engineer

Work on developing AI and machine learning algorithms, designing intelligent systems, and leveraging data analysis to solve complex problems.

Robotics Engineer

Design and develop robotic systems for various applications, such as manufacturing, healthcare, and autonomous vehicles.
