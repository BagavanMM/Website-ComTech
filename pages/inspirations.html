Andrej Karpathy

In addition to his research work, Karpathy is also an accomplished educator and communicator. He has given numerous talks and presentations on AI and has created a number of educational resources to help people understand complex topics in the field. For example, he created a series of videos called "Neural Networks: Zero to Hero," which aimed to make deep learning accessible to a wider audience by providing clear and simple explanations of complex concepts.

He is best known for his contributions to the development of convolutional neural networks (CNNs), which are the state-of-the-art algorithms used for image recognition tasks in the AI space. This groundbreaking work on CNNs was a catalyst in AI research and is a large reason why things like self-driving cars work today.

He was one of the first people who inspired me to get into the AI field and build projects in the space, and through his YouTube video tutorials, I was able to kickstart my machine learning journey.

Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso is one of my biggest inspirations because he was an artist who thought outside the box and challenged the norms of his time. Even though he faced criticism for his unconventional style, I think that he played a pretty big role in expanding the possibilities of what art could be.

His artwork was often met with mixed reactions, with some people dismissing it as mere scribbles or simplistic creations - but, I find that his ability to take everyday objects and transform them into something abstract is truly fascinating. His style also opened infinitely more interpretations to what the artwork could represent, which is beautiful.

What I admire most about Picasso is his fearlessness in pushing boundaries and exploring new artistic territory. He introduced styles like Cubism and Surrealism, which shattered the conventional notions of perspective and representation. By breaking down forms and rearranging them in unconventional ways, he challenged viewers to question their perception of reality.

His art continues to inspire me to think outside the box and to strive for artistic authenticity.

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci is one of the most iconic figures in the history of art and science. His multidisciplinary genius and insatiable curiosity continue to inspire me in countless ways.

As an artist, Leonardo's attention to detail and mastery of technique were unparalleled. His paintings, such as the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, are renowned for their beauty and depth. He captured the human form and expressions with such realism and emotion that they continue to captivate audiences centuries later.

His scientific pursuits were equally remarkable. He conducted groundbreaking studies in anatomy, engineering, and physics, among other disciplines. His sketches and inventions, like the flying machine and the Vitruvian Man, demonstrate his visionary approach and his belief in the harmony between art and science.

His ability to combine artistic expression with scientific inquiry is a testament to the power of interdisciplinary thinking and the potential for limitless exploration.

Grant Sanderson and 3blue1brown

Grant Sanderson, the creator of 3blue1brown, is an exceptional educator and mathematician who has transformed the way math is taught and understood through his captivating YouTube channel.

Through visually engaging explanations and intuitive animations, Grant has made complex mathematical concepts accessible and enjoyable for learners of all levels.

Personally, Grant's videos have inspired me to delve deeper into different mathematical topics and have sparked my curiosity to explore areas I once found intimidating.

His emphasis on intuition and problem-solving has not only improved my understanding of math but has also shaped my mindset towards problem-solving in general.

Grant Sanderson's work with 3blue1brown has left an indelible mark on the math education community, inspiring learners worldwide to embrace the wonders of mathematics and pursue further exploration in this fascinating field.
