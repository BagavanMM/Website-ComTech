Portrait Photography Project

Objective:

The objective of this portrait photography project is to explore various portrait strategies and techniques, utilizing Photoshop for enhancing and creating impactful images. The project will consist of six different portrait strategies, with two images for each strategy.

1. Partial Black and White:

Partial black and white strategy

  • Accentuate a single color while desaturating the rest
  • Use Photoshop to filter and trace desired colors
  • Shoot in a darker environment for enhanced color pop

2. Traditional Black and White:

Traditional black and white strategy

  • Capture close-up shots of people or objects
  • Use Photoshop to adjust lighting and convert to black and white

3. Environmental Portrait:

Environmental portrait strategy

  • Slightly desaturate surroundings to emphasize the subject
  • Use Photoshop to adjust brightness and contrast
  • Capture interesting backgrounds

4. Unusual Perspective Human Portrait:

Unusual perspective human portrait strategy

  • Focus on a specific feature of the person
  • Use object framing and Photoshop to remove distractions
  • Adjust lighting and color for desired effect

5. Foreground Interest Compositional Portrait:

Foreground interest compositional portrait strategy

  • Capture subjects with chaotic backgrounds
  • Utilize busy cafeteria or outdoor scenes

6. Self Portrait:

Self-portrait strategy for each group member

  • Jacky: Capture self gazing upwards, potentially in black and white
  • Bagavan: Wear all black against a white background for a black and white image

Reflection

Overall, I had an amazing experience working on this project. It was a fun and exciting journey, from the planning stage to capturing the photographs. One of the key lessons I learned during this unit was the significant impact of lighting on the outcome of the shots. I realized that proper lighting can drastically transform the look and feel of an image. I faced challenges with one of my photos due to poor lighting, and even in post-processing with Photoshop, I struggled to convey the intended message. By focusing on lighting during the initial stage, not only can we save time during editing, but the overall process becomes much smoother.

Another valuable lesson I learned was the challenge of translating the ideas and sketches in my mind into real-world photographs. Constraints such as timing and logistical limitations can make it difficult to precisely replicate the initial concept. However, my teammate and I were able to adapt and overcome these barriers, striving to closely replicate our envisioned ideas.
